Katherine G. Johnson

My area of expertise and research lies in Deep Reinforcement Learning applied to industrial processes and robotics. As part of my research, I believe it is crucial to reflect on the ethical implications that arise from the implementation and utilization of these AI techniques.

In addition to my professional pursuits, I am an enthusiastic triathlete who recognizes and appreciates the importance of maintaining a balance between work and physical fitness.
This combination of skills and interests allows me to bring a unique perspective and dedication to my work that is both well-rounded and driven.

Ph.D. Thesis Topic

My Ph.D. ultimate aim is to suggest a solution that can efficiently address the transfer knowledge problem between a deep reinforcement learning agent trained in a virtual environment and another one deployed in a real environment. The industrial assets we are using to test the algorithms are two roboic arms, the IRB120 (ABB) and the UR3e (Universal Robots).

Beyond the basics

Find some other interesting and lesser-known facts about myself.

  • Representative of the IIT Research Assistants

    For two consecutive years (since September 2021), I have been elected as the representative of research assistants at the Institute for Research in Technology (IIT).
    My responsibility is to stand up for the interests of research assistants and communicate their concerns to the IIT Board of Directors. I am also a link between senior researchers and junior assistants, ensuring that everyone's voices are heard and their needs are met.

  • Delegate of the Doctorate Program in Engineering Systems Modeling and the Comillas International Doctorate School

    In these roles, which I appointed in 2021 and 2022 respectively, I collaborate closely with the School directors to advocate for the issues and aspirations of Ph.D candidates and propose enhancements to the Ph.D programs.
    As in my role at IIT, it is a privilege to be part of these organizations and to have the opportunity to contribute to their development.

  • Lab Teacher

    Starting from January 2021, I have been a Lab teacher for the first-year Industrial Automation Master's Course of the Official Master's Degree in Industrial Engineering.
    I have helped students gain hands-on experience in wired automation systems and process control using an industrial programmable logic controller.

  • Co-Supervisor of the 2021 ICAI Best Master's Thesis Award

    The title of the master's thesis was “Improvement of alarm and video surveillance systems by incorporating audio analysis” and it focused on researching and developing a deep learning algorithm that utilizes environmental audio translated to images using Mel Spectogram to reduce the number of wrong alerts.

  • Associate of the Industry 4.0 Observatory

    Recently, I was honored to become a member of the Spanish Industry 4.0 Observatory, whose aim is to establish a permanent platform for discussion, training, research, information dissemination, and opinions on Industry 4.0.

  • Project Co-Founder

    From 2016 to 2020, I was actively involved in a start-up project called "Eiximenis - Sustainable Street Lighting". The purpose was to optimize the energy consumption of street lights based on the street occupation. Working on this project was a valuable learning experience that taught me technical skills and soft skills that are essential in the business world.
