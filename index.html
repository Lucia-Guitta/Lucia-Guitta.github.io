My area of expertise and research lies in Deep Reinforcement Learning applied to industrial processes and robotics. As part of my research, I believe it is crucial to reflect on the ethical implications that arise from the implementation and utilization of these AI techniques.

In addition to my professional pursuits, I am an enthusiastic triathlete who recognizes and appreciates the importance of maintaining a balance between work and physical fitness.
This combination of skills and interests allows me to bring a unique perspective and dedication to my work that is both well-rounded and driven.

Ph.D. Thesis Topic

My Ph.D. ultimate aim is to suggest a solution that can efficiently address the transfer knowledge problem between a deep reinforcement learning agent trained in a virtual environment and another one deployed in a real environment. The industrial assets we are using to test the algorithms are two roboic arms, the IRB120 (ABB) and the UR3e (Universal Robots).

Research Assistant projects

I have been involved in multiple projects as a research assistant for prominent companies in the industrial sector. These projects encompass a wide range of subject matters, including data analysis, the application of machine learning and deep learning techniques to resolve quality issues, as well as the development of integrated control systems and classical computer vision for mobile robots.

Sports and fitness

Sports have always been a big part of my life, from playing soccer, volleyball or golf to taking up OCR races as the Spartan Race. It wasn't until last year that I discovered my enthusiasm for triathlon. Since then, I have been training rigorously and participating in races regularly. I am proud to have won the Barcelona SuperSprint Championship in October 2022, but I know that there is still a lot of room for improvement. My ultimate goal is to finish an IRONMAN Race, and I am determined to keep pushing myself to achieve this dream.
